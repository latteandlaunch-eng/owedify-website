Welcome to the Owedify blog

If you have ever opened a bill that looked wrong, or a letter that said your claim was denied, you know the feeling: you are fairly sure you have a case, but you have no idea what to do next. This blog is here for that moment.

Owedify makes do-it-yourself kits that help you write your own letter to dispute a charge or appeal a decision. The kits do the heavy lifting on structure and wording. This blog covers everything around them: the plain background, the deadlines, and the small steps that are easy to miss.

What you will find here

We are going to publish short, practical guides on three areas where people lose money simply because the process feels confusing:

  • Medical bills. How to read an itemized bill, spot a charge that looks off, and ask the billing office to correct it.
  • Denied claims. What an appeal actually is, where to send it, and how to find the deadline printed on your own notice.
  • Credit report errors. How to request your reports, identify an item worth disputing, and write to each bureau (the companies that hold your credit file).

How we write

Every guide is meant to be read once, on a phone, while you are stressed. We use plain words, we name the deadline and the cost, and we explain any term the first time it appears. You stay in control the whole way. Owedify never sends anything for you. You write the letter, you review it, and you decide when to send it.

That is the whole idea. More guides are on the way. If there is a topic you want us to cover, write to us at hello@owedify.com.
