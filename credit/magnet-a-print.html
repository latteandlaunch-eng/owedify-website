Step 1

The only real site

Go to AnnualCreditReport.com. It is the federally authorized source; every other "free report" site is trying to sell you something. All three reports, Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion, free, every week, forever.

If a site asks for a credit card, you are on the wrong site.

Step 2

Pull all three

Errors often appear on one report and not the others. Ten minutes gets you all three; save or print each one.

Step 3

The 8 errors to look for

  • An account you never opened
  • A delinquency date that moved (re aging)
  • A balance or status that is wrong
  • A medical collection under $500, those should not appear at all
  • A payment marked late that was on time
  • A hard inquiry you never authorized
  • The same debt listed twice
  • Anything older than about 7 years
Your right, and their deadline

Found something? You can dispute it free, and the bureau has about 30 days to investigate. Items that cannot be verified must be corrected or removed.

About 1 in 5 people had an error on a credit report that was fixed after they disputed it. Source: Federal Trade Commission.
