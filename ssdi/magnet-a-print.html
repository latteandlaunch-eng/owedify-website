Denied? Your 60 day clock started

What to do next, and the three mistakes that cost people winnable appeals.

The deadline

You have about 60 days from the date on your denial notice to appeal, plus 5 days for mailing. Find the date on your notice and write your deadline here:

Avoid these

The three fatal mistakes

  1. 1Missing the deadline. Nothing else matters if you miss it. Put it in your calendar today.
  2. 2Filing a new application instead of appealing. It feels easier, but it resets everything and can forfeit the back pay an appeal protects.
  3. 3Not responding. Appeals stall when letters go unanswered. Keep every appointment and answer every request, even while you wait.
The evidence

What actually wins

Consistent medical evidence, and a day to day record. Start a simple symptom journal today: the date, what you felt, and what it stopped you from doing. Judges read these, and a year of honest entries is evidence no form can replace.

Most first applications are denied, and many people who appeal are approved later in the process. The difference is rarely the disability. It is the deadline, the paperwork, and the follow through.
