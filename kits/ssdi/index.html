How the appeal works

Four chances, not one.

A denial is not the final word. Social Security gives you four levels of appeal, and each one is a fresh look at your case. The kit prepares the right request for whichever level you are on.

1

Reconsideration

SSA-561

Someone who was not part of the first decision reviews your full file again. The kit drafts this request from your answers.

2

Hearing before a judge

HA-501

You appear before an administrative law judge who looks at everything fresh, in person or by video, instead of a paper review.

The largest share of approvals happen at this level
3

Appeals Council review

HA-520

If the hearing did not go your way, the council checks the judge's decision for legal or procedural mistakes.

4

Federal court

The final step. A judge outside Social Security reviews your case. The kit helps you keep your paperwork in order long before you ever get here.

65days to file

The countdown starts the day listed on your notice.

You get 60 days to appeal, plus 5 for mailing, from the date printed on your denial. Miss it and you usually have to start the whole claim over. The kit counts the days for you and helps you add the deadline to your own calendar so your phone reminds you.

What wins the case

Your medical records are the most important part.

Appeals are won on evidence, not on how strongly you argue.

The kit shows you which records matter most: recent treatment notes, test results, and anything that documents how your condition limits a normal workday. It keeps a simple checklist so nothing is missing when you file, and reminds you to keep getting care while you wait.

Not sure a record helps? Ask your doctor's office or call your local Social Security office, it is a normal question.
