Step one · the deadline

Your appeal deadline, calculated to the day.

Miss it and you can lose the right to appeal at all.

Most plans give you up to 180 days to file a first appeal, but the date that governs your case is the one printed on your own denial notice. You enter that date, and the kit works out exactly how many days you have left and adds the due date to your phone's calendar with a reminder.

Everything runs on your own device, so the reminder comes from your calendar, not from us.

Step two · the letters

A complete appeal letter, written from your answers.

No blank page, no legal jargon to figure out.

Answer a short set of questions: what was denied, why you believe it was wrong, and the amount, and the kit drafts your dispute, appeal, and negotiation letters in firm, specific language. It cites the rule that fits your situation, like the No Surprises Act for an out-of-network emergency bill, so your letter reads like it came from someone who knows the process.

Every letter is yours to read, edit, copy, save, or print, and you send it yourself.
